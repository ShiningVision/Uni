Exam 2, 25.01.2022, nominal start 09:45, latest end 11:05, after starting you have at most 70 minutes

Geöffnet: Dienstag, 25. Januar 2022, 09:45
Geschlossen: Dienstag, 25. Januar 2022, 11:05

Exam procedure

  • The exam takes place online in Moodle in your browser. We recommend Google Chrome (or Chromium), Javascript must be enabled, turn off popup blocker.
  • You can take the exam on 25.01.2022 between 09:45 and 11:05 and have a maximum of 70 minutes to complete it.
  • The exam contains several tasks. Some of them contain subtasks. These can usually be addressed independently of each other; make sure that you address all subtasks and clearly state which answer relates to which subtask. The answer templates will help you to do this.
  • A maximum of 25 points can be gained in total.
  • Partial points will be awarded for partially correct answers. If parts of your answers contradict each other or are not suitable, it becomes likely that not all points can be awarded. A mass copy&paste of possible answers or excerpts from the documents is therefore usually counterproductive - also because the answers usually require the contents of SE2 to be sensibly applied on a given scenario. Your answers and arguments should therefore be appropriate for and match to these scenarios.
  • Tasks with open answers usually include an expected answer length (typically 1-2 sentences), this is usually sufficient. Longer answers are technically possible, but usually not necessary.

Overview

  • Time: The end of the exam is a "hard" deadline. Example: If an exam closes at 13:15 and you do not start until 13:00, you will only have 15 minutes to complete the exam.
  • Preparing answers - Recommendation: Do not answer tasks directly in Moodle, but locally in an editor on your device, and then copy the answers into the exam answer fields provided by Moodle (do not use SMILEYS; no special characters, etc.). We recommend the same for multiple choice tasks. Better safe than sorry: Store the given answers on your device as well.
  • The exam mode was communicated to you before the exam. With participation in the examination you have accepted the examination mode.
  • By taking this exam, you declare on oath that you will take this exam independently, without the help of third parties and without unauthorized aids.
    • Your exam may be subject to a plagiarism check for verification purposes.
    • Till the end of the course and beyond within the assessment period of four weeks, the examiner may also make oral questions on the subject matter of the examination. This can also be done on a random basis.
    • If unauthorised aids are used and/or the exam is not written independently, the exam will not be assessed and will be documented with an X in the transcript.
    • The exam is an individual work. You may use the information stored or referenced in the SE2 Moodle course during the exam. Other aids are not permitted.
  • If the exam is not attempted within the defined exam-timespan using Moodle, it will be graded with 0 points. In case of technical problems, please contact the course administration immediately. For this please use the following e-mail address: se2@swa.univie.ac.at.

Comprehension 

  • Unclear words or tasks: If you are not aware of a word's/task's  meaning you can contact us using se2@swa.univie.ac.at. Describe your request and the related task briefly so that we can deduce for which of the tasks you would like to ask us a question. The order of the tasks presented to you is random and therefore does hardly help us at this task. Please tackle other tasks during waiting periods.  
  • For the question: Is my answer correct? As with normal exams, no information about this can be given during the exam. 

Technical Issues

  • In case of technical issues please contact the course administration immediately. For this please use the following e-mail address: se2@swa.univie.ac.at.
