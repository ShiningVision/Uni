Frage 5
Vollständig
Erreichte Punkte 0,30 von 1,50


Fragetext
Do you agree with the following statements? Explain in 1-2 sentences for each why, or why not.
Statement 1: A development process that strives for higher software quality redistributes resources away from early quality assurance activities into post-implementation debugging and refactoring.
Statement 2: Post-implementation quality assurance activities have more leverage on product quality than earlier quality assurance activities, so it doesn’t make sense to invest much time in early quality assurance activities.
Statement 3: The net effects of the above approach are fewer defects and shorter development time, but overall it can lead to higher costs since the design and evolution of a software-quality program has a high overhead.



Antworttext
Statement 1: TRUE / FALSE
Explanation: FALSE, they should redistribute resources away from post-implementation debugging into early quality assurance.

Statement 2: TRUE / FALSE
Explanation: FALSE, it is easier to correct mistakes in the early stages than in the post-implementation stage.

Statement 3: TRUE / FALSE
Explanation:TRUE, the more time is dedicated into early quality assurance, the easier it is to debug and add new features later on.

Vollständig
Erreichte Punkte 1,00 von 6,00


Fragetext
An insurance company wants to manage its customers and the insurance policies that they have ordered, and track the payments they have made for each policy. If a customer has not paid up on time for a policy, a warning must be sent using the contact data they have provided.

Write a minimal code example that implements the above and violates both the separation of concerns and information hiding principles, and then explain briefly how you would need to refactor it to correct all the violations.

Antworttext
class Customer{
public String name;
public String contactInfo;

public List<Insurance> policies;
public PaymentState getInsurancePolicyPaymentState(Insurance);
public void sendWarning(){
      warn(contactInfo);
}
}


To correct the violation of separation of concern, I would split Customer up into classes Customer, CustomerAccount. CustomerAccount then holds a customer instance and the insurance policies.

To correct the violation of information hiding principles I would set name, contactInfo and policies private and make getter and if needed setter functions.
